About

In my current position at the UF Honey Bee Research and Extension Lab, I conduct research on the efficacy of chemical controls for Varroa destructor and small hive beetles, among other pests. I also serve as the lab's data analyst, and have analyzed data and written for over a dozen experiments so far.

Previously, I earned a Master of Science in Ecology from the University of Georgia, where I worked with the Altizer Lab. My thesis sought to determine the sub-lethal effects of clothianidin and imidacloprid on larval and adult monarch butterflies.

Research

Testing novel chemicals against Varroa destructor and small hive beetles

Currently, the major research that I conduct hope to test the efficacy of chemicals against honey bee pests, while ensuring safety to honey bee colonies. Our laboratory research is centered around the EPA's tier 1 tests, which we hope will inform candidates for field experiments.

Effects of neonicotinoids on monarch butterflies

My Master's thesis was centered around a class of insecticides called neonicotinoids, which have been cited as one of the causes for decline in monarch populations. We tested the effects of neonicotinoids on both larval and adult monarchs, and found that the effects could depend on the species of milkweed larval monarchs are reared on. For more information, here is the publication.
